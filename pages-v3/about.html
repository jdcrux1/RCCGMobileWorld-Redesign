Our Story

A Church Born from Vision

The Redeemed Christian Church of God (RCCG) began with a simple vision: to reach every soul for Christ and establish churches in every nation. What started as a small gathering has grown into a worldwide movement touching millions of lives.

Our journey reflects God's faithfulness through decades of ministry, growth, and community transformation. From our humble beginnings to establishing major landmarks, every step has been guided by God's purpose.

Today, RCCG Mobile World continues this legacy, embracing technology and innovation to reach the next generation while staying rooted in biblical truth and authentic community.

What We Believe

Our Core Convictions

The foundation of our faith that guides everything we do.

The Bible

God's Word is truth and our guide for life.

We believe the Bible is God's inspired Word, without error in its original manuscripts. It is the final authority for faith and practice. We interpret it in context, seeking its timeless truth for daily living.

Salvation

Grace through faith in Jesus Christ.

We believe salvation is a free gift through faith in Jesus Christ. His death and resurrection provide forgiveness and eternal life for all who believe. No work can earn what grace freely gives.

Love

God's love transforms everything.

We believe God's love is the foundation of our faith. It compels us to love one another, serve our community, and share the Good News. Love is not just a feeling—it's action.

The Church

Community matters to God.

We believe the Church is the body of Christ, called to worship God together, equip believers, and reach the world. No Christian should walk alone—we grow better together.

Holy Spirit

God's presence empowers us.

We believe the Holy Spirit empowers believers for service, gifts for ministry, and produces fruit in our lives. He guides, comforts, and transforms us into Christ's image.

Eternity

Our future is secured in Christ.

We believe in the bodily resurrection of all people—everlasting life for believers and everlasting separation from God for unbelievers. Our hope is not in this life but in the life to come.
